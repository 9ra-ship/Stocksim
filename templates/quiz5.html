Quiz: Stock Market Psychology

1. What does "herd behavior" mean in stock market psychology?
  • Following the majority of traders without independent analysis
  • Following a predetermined trading plan
  • Selling stocks during a bull market
  • Trading based on technical indicators only
2. Which psychological bias makes investors hold on to losing stocks too long?
  • Loss aversion
  • Overconfidence bias
  • Confirmation bias
  • Anchoring
3. What is overconfidence bias in trading?
  • Overestimating one’s ability to predict market movements
  • Believing the market will always go up
  • Relying on expert opinions
  • Avoiding high-risk investments
4. Which term refers to the fear of missing out on a profitable trade?
  • FOMO
  • Loss aversion
  • Anchoring
  • Herding
5. What is "anchoring" in stock market psychology?
  • Fixating on past prices or information when making trading decisions
  • Following a trend without proper research
  • Selling stocks based on emotions
  • Ignoring technical analysis
6. What is the "recency bias" in trading?
  • Focusing more on recent events than on long-term data
  • Trusting the advice of experts
  • Relying on historical data for decision-making
  • Avoiding investment risk based on past losses
7. What is the "confirmation bias" in stock trading?
  • Seeking information that confirms pre-existing beliefs
  • Avoiding new investment opportunities
  • Believing only in technical analysis
  • Following advice from financial news networks
8. Which bias can lead to an investor ignoring negative information?
  • Confirmation bias
  • Loss aversion
  • Hindsight bias
  • Herd behavior
9. What does the "availability bias" cause an investor to do?
  • Overestimate the importance of information that is most readily available
  • Rely on mathematical models for predictions
  • Ignore past data when making trading decisions
  • Focus on long-term strategies instead of short-term gains
10. What psychological phenomenon makes investors continue investing in a losing stock?
  • Sunk cost fallacy
  • Overconfidence bias
  • Herd behavior
  • Availability bias
11. What does the "status quo bias" cause an investor to do?
  • Prefer the current state of affairs and avoid change
  • Take risks without proper analysis
  • Seek new investment opportunities
  • React emotionally to market fluctuations
12. What is "loss aversion" in stock market psychology?
  • The tendency to prefer avoiding losses over acquiring gains
  • Trading on impulse based on emotions
  • Believing that the market is always efficient
  • Relying on historical performance for predictions
13. What is the "bandwagon effect"?
  • The tendency to follow others when making investment decisions
  • Overestimating one's ability to predict market outcomes
  • Selling stocks too early due to panic
  • Investing based on market trends without research
14. What does "recency bias" lead investors to do?
  • Place too much weight on recent events and trends
  • Trust the opinions of financial experts
  • Follow the crowd without analysis
  • Focus on long-term trends and data
15. What is the "endowment effect"?
  • The tendency to value something more once we own it
  • Believing that the market will recover after losses
  • Focusing on short-term gains over long-term strategies
  • Failing to diversify investments
16. What is "mental accounting" in stock market psychology?
  • Treating money differently depending on its source or intended use
  • Avoiding high-risk investments
  • Overreacting to market fluctuations
  • Trading without a clear plan
17. What does the "illusion of control" refer to?
  • Believing that one can control or influence outcomes in an uncertain environment
  • Believing that the market will always go up
  • Trusting past performance over future predictions
  • Relying on expert advice without critical thinking
18. What does "self-attribution bias" lead an investor to do?
  • Take credit for success while blaming failure on external factors
  • Invest impulsively based on emotions
  • Trust the market’s trends
  • Seek advice from experts before making decisions
19. What is the "framing effect" in stock market decisions?
  • Making decisions based on how information is presented
  • Overreacting to negative news
  • Following market trends
  • Relying on past performance to predict future outcomes
20. What does the "optimism bias" cause an investor to do?
  • Overestimate the likelihood of positive market outcomes
  • Rely too heavily on historical performance for predictions
  • Avoid high-risk investments
  • Invest without proper research
